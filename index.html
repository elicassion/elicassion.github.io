Dynamic Detection of Communities and Their Evolutions in Temporal Social Networks

Submitted to AAAI'18

A community in networks is thought as a cluster of nodes with more connections amongst its members than between its members and outside nodes. Dynamic community detection sometimes can be considered as detecting community evolution task.Recently a variety of community detection methods have enjoyed impressive success. However, most of them are limited to static social networks, and the dynamic community detection methods use snapshots to divide time into slices, which results in a loss of more detailed change in a cluster.In this paper, we present a new model, Community Detection Over Time (CDOT), which can explore the community evolution in a dynamic social network through modeling the temporal strength between communities and users. Rather than snapshots, we use Normal distribution to estimate the strength, which is concise in structure and more comprehensive.We use prediction method and analysis applications to evaluate our model and extensive experiments on large social data sets show our improvement in prediction accuracy and CDOT reveals distinctive insight in our society.

Tracking Evolution of an Academic Field

Ongoing

There are many academic fields thriving in academia. With researchers and tons of their paper contributing to their professional fields, academic conceptions, problems and corresponding solutions are completed by deeper researches and are broken through by the presentations of innovative methods. Under this circumstance, a timeline composed by significant conceptions and paper is necessary for a scientific research novice to grasp the evolution of a field. In this paper, I propose using a network model to track the evolution and even make predictions, which will give insights to both novice and veteran researchers.
