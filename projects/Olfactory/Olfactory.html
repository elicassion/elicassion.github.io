Olfactory Perception

An E-nose to sense and reproduce aromas helping people remind and express perfume they once met with.

HardWare Crafting

First, we need hardware to catch those aromas. I designed the hardware box with collaborators major in industrial design. The box has three layers: The first layer on the top is the space for attaching the sensor array. The second layer in the middle, which occupies the majority of the room, serves as a diffusion space for sample perfumes exposing to the sensor array. The sample is placed on a metal plate at the bottom of this layer. There is a heating element attached to the other side of the plate which is intended to speed up the diffusion. The last layer is a space for wires going out. There are also two windows with fans for control the air flow in the box. When collecting data, windows and fans are closed. When it ends, partitions are removed to open the window and fans are activated to exhaust the perfume. The material of the box is carefully selected which has extremely low absorption ability preventing aroma residues that affect continuous usage.

Data Collection

The sensor array and the heating element are connected to an Arduino module, and then the Arduino module communicates with a PC trough USB serial port. A pre-designed heating logic is programmed in the Arduino module. I designed main control program to drive the experiment procedures and collect data from the sensor array. The program combines Python and C++. I also designed a real-time visualization to see the data we get.

Analysis

We ran experiments collecting data from all kinds of available perfumes. Also, we send these samples to Department of Chemistry to get their mass spectrometry analysis result to figure out which kinds of chemical compounds are in their. Combine with the data from both sources, I use algorithm to analyze the similarity of perfumes, resulting in a similarity matrix of perfumes. The darker means more similar.
